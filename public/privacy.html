Privacy Policy

Last updated July 2026

The Hourly ("the app") is a private, invitation-only photo journal. People join private groups and share hourly photos with the other members of those groups. This policy explains what information the app collects, how it is used, who it is shared with, and the choices you have. It applies to everyone who uses the app.

The app is operated by an individual ("we," "us"). For any privacy question, or to exercise the rights described below, contact akram.aboukhalil@gmail.com. For the purposes of the EU/UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), this operator is the data controller.

What we collect

  • Account information: your name and email address, obtained either from Google Sign-In or from the email address you enter to receive a sign-in link.
  • Activity information: your timezone, your device's push-notification subscription (so we can notify you), and the last time you were active (used to show who is currently online).
  • Photos you upload: the image itself, and — only when your device grants location permission, or when the photo file contains embedded GPS data — the GPS coordinates, the approximate city, and the local weather (temperature and humidity) at that location and time.
  • Comments and reactions: any comments or emoji reactions you leave on photos.
  • Preferences: settings such as temperature unit, time format, theme, and notification choices.

Location data

Location is optional. If you deny your browser's location prompt and your photos do not contain embedded GPS data, no coordinates are collected and photos are stored without a location. When coordinates are available, they are sent to two third-party services to enrich the photo (see "Third-party services" below): a weather provider and a mapping/geocoding provider. If you do not want a photo's location shared, deny location permission and remove embedded GPS/EXIF data from the image before uploading.

How we use your information

We use the information above only to operate the app's core features: letting invited members of a group share and view each other's hourly photos, see where and when a photo was taken, comment and react, and receive notifications. We do not use your information for advertising, profiling, behavioral tracking, or any purpose beyond running the app. We do not sell or rent personal information, and we do not use third-party analytics or advertising trackers.

Legal bases (EU/UK users)

Where GDPR applies, we process your information on the basis of: your consent (for example, location data and push notifications, which you can withdraw at any time); the performance of our agreement with you to provide the service (your account, photos, comments); and our legitimate interest in keeping the service secure and functioning.

Who can see your content

Photos, comments, and reactions are visible to the members of the group(s) you share them into, and to you. Your name is visible to people who share a group with you. Group membership is controlled by invite links; anyone who holds a group's invite link can join that group and see content shared into it, so share invite links only with people you trust.

Third-party services

We rely on the following service providers to run the app. Each receives only the data needed for its function:

  • Supabase — hosts our database, file storage, and authentication. Your account data, photos, comments, and reactions are stored here.
  • Google Sign-In — if you sign in with Google, Google provides us your name and email to create your account. We do not access, read, or post to any other Google data or service on your behalf.
  • Open-Meteo — when a photo has coordinates, those coordinates are sent to Open-Meteo to retrieve the local weather.
  • OpenStreetMap / Nominatim — when a photo has coordinates, those coordinates are sent to OpenStreetMap's geocoding service to look up the city name.
  • Web Push services — to deliver notifications while the app is closed, notification payloads are delivered through your device's push service (operated by Apple, Google, or Mozilla depending on your browser).

Where your data is stored and international transfers

Data is stored on Supabase's hosted infrastructure. Depending on the project's region and the third-party services above, your information may be processed in the United States or other countries whose data-protection laws may differ from those in your country. By using the app you understand that your information may be transferred to and processed in those locations.

How long we keep it

We retain your account, photos, comments, and reactions until you delete them or delete your account. Notifications and "nudges" are transient and removed after delivery. When you delete your account, your profile and associated content are removed from our database (see below); copies may persist briefly in provider backups or caches before being overwritten.

Your rights and choices

You can, at any time:

  • Access and correct your name and preferences from the app's Settings screen.
  • Delete your account, all your photos, and your comments and reactions from Settings ("Delete My Account & Photos"). This is immediate and cannot be undone.
  • Withdraw consent for location (deny the browser prompt) or notifications (turn them off in your device or app settings).
  • Request a copy of your data, or ask questions about this policy, by emailing us.

Depending on where you live (for example, the EU/UK under GDPR, or California under the CCPA/CPRA), you may have additional rights, including access, portability, correction, deletion, and the right to object to or restrict processing. We honor these requests for all users. EU/UK users also have the right to lodge a complaint with their local data-protection supervisory authority.

Security

Access to content is restricted by authentication and database row-level security so that content is served only to members of the relevant group. Data is transmitted over encrypted connections (HTTPS). No system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security; please use a secure email account and device, and share invite links carefully.

Children

The app is intended for private use among invited groups, which may include family members who are minors. It is not directed at the general public or marketed to children. If a minor uses the app, it should be with the involvement and consent of a parent or guardian, who is responsible for that use. We do not knowingly collect personal information from a child under the age of 13 (or the minimum age in your jurisdiction) without appropriate parental consent. A parent or guardian may contact us to review or delete a child's information.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by the "Last updated" date above and, where appropriate, communicated in the app. Continued use after a change means you accept the updated policy.

Contact
